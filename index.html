    About

    Hello and thanks for visiting my homepage. My name is Min-kyeong (Min) Cha, and I am a Ph.D. student at the School of Public Policy, Georgia Institute of Technology. I mainly study Energy and Environment policy.

    With my interdisciplinary background, I aim to understand what drives the electrification and clean energy transition. My research focuses on how different environmental policies affect household adoption, and possible equity concerns in those policies. I am also interested in change in consumption behavior after adoption of renewable electricity. I have worked on finding socioeconomic, attitudinal, and drivers and barriers to household adoption of clean technologies such as solar panels, electric vehicles, or efficient HVAC, and on analyzing the potential of rooftop solar in Georgia.

    I am BBISS Graduate Fellow at Georgia Institute of Technology. I was also a Fulbright student for the 2020-2022 academic year.

    I love to connect with people for exciting projects! Please do not hesitate to contact me at: mcha39 [at] gatech.edu
